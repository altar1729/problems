Union-Closed Sets Conjecture

This is probably a conjecture that lies in the heart of most combinatorialists. Suppose we have a family of sets $\mathcal{F}$ on the ground set $[n]$ which is closed under the operation of union, i.e. if $A,B\in \mathcal{F}$, then $A\cup B\in \mathcal{F}$. Then, there exists an element $i\in [n]$ which is contained in at least $\frac{|\mathcal{F}|}{2}$ sets. There has been a recent development in this conjecture by Justin Gilmer , which used very clever entropy technique to prove that there exists an element $i \in[n]$ which is contained in at least $\frac{3-\sqrt{5}}{2}|\mathcal{F}|$ many sets.
